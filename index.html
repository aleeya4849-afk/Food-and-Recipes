Cooking Tips & Tricks

Expert advice to make the most of your ingredients

How to Store Vegetables for Longer Freshness

Keep leafy greens in airtight containers with a paper towel to absorb moisture. Store root vegetables in a cool, dark place. Most vegetables last longer when stored unwashed in the refrigerator's crisper drawer.

Creative Ways to Use Stale Bread

Transform stale bread into croutons, breadcrumbs, or bread pudding. You can also make French toast, panzanella salad, or use it to thicken soups and sauces.

Freezing Tips for Leftovers

Portion leftovers before freezing for easy thawing. Use airtight containers or freezer bags to prevent freezer burn. Label everything with dates. Most cooked foods freeze well for 2-3 months.

Reviving Wilted Vegetables

Soak limp vegetables in ice water for 5-10 minutes to crisp them up. This works well for carrots, celery, and leafy greens. For slightly wilted herbs, trim the stems and place in a glass of water like a bouquet.
